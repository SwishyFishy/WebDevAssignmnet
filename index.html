Magic: The Gathering

Magic: the Gathering is my favourite game. My preferred way to play is the Commander format, which is a variant ruleset for 4 players wherein each player builds their deck around a 'Commander' which they can play again (for an extra price) if it's destroyed.

Fun Fact: I'm also the President of Trent University's Magic: the Gathering club! You can find us at trentmtgcommunity@gmail.com. We host weekly meetings for Trent students to get together on campus and play Magic, and occasionally we host free-to-enter limited events!

My Favourite MtG Decks

  • Shorikai, Genesis Engine Control

    This deck is my pride and joy. It uses its commander to draw additional cards, and nearly half of the deck is comprised of cards that I can use to defend myself. It aims to stop other players from winning, exhaust their options, and then capitalize by playing a powerful combination of cards that can win the game on the spot, but require plenty of time to set up.

  • Magar of the Magic Strings Spell Animation

    This deck uses a card with a unique ability as its commander. The eponymous Magar lets me turn my spells into creatures that cast copies of themselves when they deal damage to my opponents, letting me double (or triple, or septuple) up on obscenely powerful spells that can typically only be cast once.

  • Bosh, Iron Golem Artifact Combo

    This deck has very little capacity to interact with its opponents. Instead, it plays 30-40 cards that interact with each other in ways that can 'go infinite' - a term for when a player is able to 'loop' some of their cards to do something an arbitrary amount of times. It aims to do this to generate infinite amounts of mana, which it can use to cast and activate its commander as many times as it needs to to win the game. What makes it so much fun is that because there are so many cards that can contribute, it's often reminiscent of a jigsaw puzzle trying to assemble a combo on the fly.
